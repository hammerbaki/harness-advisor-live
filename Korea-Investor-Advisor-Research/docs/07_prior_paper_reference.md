## What the Paper Studied

The paper reports an exploratory proof-of-concept deployment of autonomous,
persona-driven AI agents across Twitter/X, Discord, and Telegram using ElizaOS.
The deployment lasted 18 days, processed 5389 interactions, and collected
feedback from 28 volunteer participants.

The paper's research questions focused on:

1. AI-agent automation across multiple platforms.
2. How character design influences user experience.
3. Technical challenges and opportunities in multi-platform deployment.

Key reported findings included platform-dependent automation effectiveness,
platform-persona alignment being more important than architectural
sophistication, and platform-specific technical performance differences.

## Relevance to the New Advisor Paper

Assessment: relevant, but as a methodological and conceptual reference, not as
domain literature for finance.

Strongly relevant themes:

- real-world PoC deployment of LLM agents
- agent architecture as a research object
- persona/role design
- platform-specific optimization
- interaction between technical performance and user adoption
- limitations of exploratory PoC methods
- need for systematic ablation and stronger evaluation

Weak or indirect relevance:

- finance domain knowledge
- investment advisory regulation
- financial RAG, DART, KRX, or IR data
- evidence-grounded numerical reasoning

## Recommended Use in the New Paper

Use this prior paper in the introduction and methodology sections to establish a
research lineage:

- Prior work demonstrated real-world feasibility of autonomous LLM agents in
  marketing automation.
- The new paper shifts from multi-platform social marketing agents to
  domain-grounded financial advisory agents.
- The earlier paper's limitation section motivates the new paper's stronger
  emphasis on reproducibility, deterministic validators, ablation, and evidence
  grounding.

Potential positioning:

> Building on prior proof-of-concept deployment work on autonomous LLM agents in
> social media marketing, this study investigates whether a similarly
> deployment-oriented methodology can be adapted to a higher-stakes financial
> information setting, where evidence grounding, updateability, and deterministic
> validation become first-order design requirements.

## How Not to Use It

Do not rely on this paper as evidence that the financial advisor works or that
users trust it in investment settings. It should not substitute for finance,
RAG, information retrieval, or decision-support literature.

## Citation Role

Recommended citation role:

- Background on deployed autonomous LLM agents.
- Methodological precedent for exploratory PoC deployment.
- Motivation for moving beyond persona/platform design toward evidence-grounded
  agent architecture.

Not recommended as:

- primary finance reference
- primary RAG reference
- evaluation benchmark for investment advisory systems
